SPINNAKER's vision is a network of professionals operating more efficiently and innovating more quickly as a result of a shared information commons on savings products for the poor.

About SPINNAKER
The Global Assets Program at the New America Foundation has been contracted by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation to collaboratively create with leaders in the savings field an online platform and network to achieve this vision. The SPINNAKER Network aims to provide practitioners and researchers with an online platform that aggregates data and information on savings, engages leaders in field, and highlights innovative savings products to funders, researchers, practitioners, and policymakers in the savings for the poor field from around the world.

Partners

Collaborative sharing is at the core of SPINNAKER's mission to catalogue (and spur new) data, research and analysis on savings products for the poor. As such, we invite other practitioners, researchers and stakeholders in the field to join and contribute to SPINNAKER based on their own interests and expertise. Why Savings for the Poor?

The "Savings for the Poor" field is growing and evolving rapidly as various efforts from leaders in the field have led to the creation, development and evaluation of new products, projects, and policies that aim to promote effective financial inclusion. Nonetheless, successfully designing and delivering sustainable and appropriate savings services to bottom of the pyramid markets is notoriously challenging.


SPINNAKER works to provide a knowledge base from which we can collectively and collaboratively discuss and address pertinent issues related to product design and delivery of savings services for the poor. Ultimately, SPINNAKER's goal is to spur innovation and achieve greater savings mobilization through breaking down silos and leveraging the collective work, ideas, and information of the field.

Savings Products

SPINNAKER will aim to achieve its goals by collecting, analyzing and discussing data on formal savings services with the following baseline characteristics:

  • Designed for and/or accessible to low-income, unbanked or financially excluded clients, frequently referred to as Bottom of the Pyramid markets
  • Offered by formal, regulated financial institutions
  • Policies such as interest rates and fees are set by the institution as opposed to individual savers

In a collaborative effort, we have created a data form (which can be downloaded by clicking here) to better understand and identify what features and characteristics are key to a saving product's success. We invite your feedback on how we can continue to improve it.

Innovation

There are several different ways of defining innovation. Research shows that a savings product innovation can result from improving the extent to which a product is: "liquid, private, clear, convenient, well-known, viable, affordable, and addresses psychological and emotional elements" (GAFIS Focus Note 1, Bankable Frontier, Feb. 2011). Innovations can also be understood as a product feature that responds to client demand identified through market research (Ashraf, et. al., ERD Technical Note Series, Asian Development Bank, Nov. 2003). Others have examined reasons for low usage rates as areas for institutions to innovate, such as proximity and security (Deshpande, Focus Note 37, CGAP, Sept. 2006). Through knowledge sharing and collaboration, SPINNAKER will provide a space where creative and successful practices and ideas are shared and explored among this diverse and growing field, with the ultimate goal of spurring innovation that helps achieve the fieldâ€™s common objectives more effectively and rapidly.
